REMOVING OBJECTS EXERCISE

To wrap up this lesson, you will each work on a unique image to remove all of the people and various other items in the photo. All of the images you can choose from are similar to the Colosseum image we used in these directions – they are all aerial shots that include tourists, vehicles, and so forth. Follow the directions below:

  1. Scroll down this page and look at the images - there are 50 different images to choose from
  2. When you find a photo you want to use, you can simply right-click the picture, click Save As, and save the image to your Remove Objects folder

If you wish to see the full sized version of the image, simply click on it and the image will open in a new window. Note that some images are VERY LARGE. This was done on purpose to allow you to zoom in very close to make adjustments. Note also that some images are actually smaller than what is displayed below. This is simply because the image was small to begin with, so choose your image carefully.

Some things to keep in mind about the images below:

  • These pictures were selected because they all had some people (and other objects) in them that can be easily removed

    •

  • Some images are easier to work with than others

  • If you completed this tutorial quickly (or already know how to remove objects in Photoshop/Photopea) challenge yourself by choosing an image that is more complicated to work with or will take more time

  • Below each image is the name of the building/location in the image including the city, in case you want to look up additional image to use as source material

  • Below each image is also a short instruction indicating what you will need to remove from that particular image - read these instruction carefully as they will guide your work

  • Be sure that you remove what is indicated - you can remove more objects, but don't remove less than indicated

Keep in mind here that your goal is for the location to look abandoned and devoid of people. Try your best to make the final result look as realistic as possible - this is what you will be graded on. If it is easy to tell that people/objects have been removed, you will lose points. Take your time and do a good job.

  1. Use any of the tools/techniques that we covered in this lesson to remove whatever is indicated in the instructions below the image of your choice
