REMOVING OBJECTS EXERCISE

To wrap up this lesson, you will each work on a unique image to remove all of the people and various other items in the photo. All of the images you can choose from are similar to the Colosseum image we used in these directions - they are all aerial shots that include tourists, vehicles, and so forth.

Follow the directions below:

  1. Scroll down this page and look at the images - there are 50 different images to choose from
  2. When you find a photo you want to use, let the teacher know so that image can be reserved for you

Some important things to keep in mind about the images:

  • Every student must work on a different image - no two students can use the same photo
  • You MUST get your image choice approved by the teacher - be aware that your choice may be rejected if another student has already chosen it
  • If you completed this tutorial quickly (or already know how to remove objects in Photoshop/Photopea) challenge yourself by choosing an image that is more complicated to work with or will take more time - this will help you practice your skills and become more proficient
  • Some images are easier to work with than others - choose wisely!
  • DO NOT start working until your image has been approved
  1. Once your image has been approved, you can simply right-click the picture, click Save As, and save the image to your Remove Objects folder

If you wish to see the full sized version of the image, simply click on it and the image will open in a new window. Note that some images are VERY LARGE. This was done on purpose to allow you to zoom and easily make adjustments. Note also that some images are actually smaller than what is displayed below. This is simply because the image was small to begin with, so choose your image carefully.

Some things to keep in mind about the images below:

  • These pictures were selected because they all had some people (and other objects) in them that can be easily removed
  • If you completed this tutorial quickly (or already know how to remove objects in Photoshop/Photopea) challenge yourself by choosing an image that is more complicated to work with or will take more time
  • Below each image is the name of the building/location in the image including the city, in case you want to look up additional images to use as source material to make changes
  • Below each image is also a short instruction indicating what you will need to remove from that particular image - read these instruction carefully as they will guide your work
  • Be sure that you remove what is indicated - you can remove more objects, but don't remove less than indicated

Keep in mind that your goal for this exercise is for the location to look abandoned and devoid of people. Try your best to make the final result look as realistic as possible - this is what you will be graded on. If it is easy to tell that people/objects have been removed, you will lose points. Take your time and do a good job.

  1. Use any of the tools/techniques that we covered in this lesson to remove whatever is indicated in the instructions below the image of your choice
  2. When you are finished with your image, save it as EAremove_yourlastname (replace yourlastname with your actual last name so I know that it is yours)
  3. Submit your image to be graded

When choosing an image, you may be tempted to go with the one that has the fewest people in it to remove. Let me warn you - don't use that as a criteria for selecting your image. Images with few people will have additional objects in them that you must remove. I'll be straight with you here - some images are easier to work with than others. This is because some of you are more adept at working with Photopea than others. If you have the hang of this removing objects stuff, choose an image that is a little more complicated so you can advance your skills. If you are having a more difficult time getting things to look good, don't worry about it - simply go for an image that is easier to work with. Make sure you read carefully what needs to be removed in each image before making your choice, and look closely at the image to see WHAT TOOLS will work best for that image.

Remember, you are shooting for REALISM, so take your time and make it look good.
